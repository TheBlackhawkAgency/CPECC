About the Council

The China Pakistan Economic Corridor Council (CPECC) is registered under the Societies Registration Act, 1860, and is headquartered in Islamabad, Pakistan. The Council functions as a platform for facilitating bilateral cooperation between Pakistan and China, with a focus on long-term development, institutional collaboration, and public benefit initiatives.

Its core mandate is to promote structured engagement in education, research, healthcare, and social development sectors.

Vision Statement

To establish a leading institutional platform that promotes sustainable development, strategic cooperation, and knowledge exchange between Pakistan and China for long-term socio-economic progress.

Mission Statement

To facilitate structured initiatives in education, research, healthcare, and community development through partnerships, innovation, and inclusive programs aimed at improving quality of life.

Strategic Objectives

Bilateral Cooperation

To enhance institutional and people-to-people linkages between Pakistan and China through collaborative frameworks.

Education & Research Development

To support establishment of academic institutions, research centers, and professional training programs.

Healthcare & Social Welfare

To contribute toward improved healthcare access and welfare support for underserved communities.

Community Development

To develop infrastructure for education, literacy, and public awareness at community level.

Programs and Initiatives

The Council undertakes academic collaborations, policy dialogues, international conferences, healthcare projects, research publications, and capacity-building initiatives aimed at sustainable national development.

Governance and Accountability

CPECC is governed by an executive committee comprising experienced professionals and stakeholders. The organization follows a transparent and accountable governance framework ensuring ethical management of all operations.
